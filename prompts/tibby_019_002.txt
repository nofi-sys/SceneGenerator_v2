The literary style of this scene is dramatic. The tone is intense and emotional, and the point of view is from Alice's perspective.

- Mark comes home to find Alice terrified and on the verge of a breakdown
- Alice is afraid of the spirits she sees and hears, which she believes are real
- Mark tries to reason with her and convince her that it is all in her head
- He tells her that his sister is coming to visit and she will be a good distraction
- Alice is relieved and agrees to try and get better

Mark: husband of Alice, represses his words, concerned for her mental state
Alice: wife of Mark, unbalanced mind, nervous prostration, believes in spirits
Elinor: sister of Mark, wealthy, love of society
 Russell: man who came to their door, serpent who entered their Eden, villain
