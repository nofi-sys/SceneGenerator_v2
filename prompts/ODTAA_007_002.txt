Donna Emilia is upset about the political situation in the country, and Hi tries to comfort her. Rosa tells Hi about the censorship of letters and how it has been affecting their family.

The literary style of this scene is very formal. The tone is serious and somber, and the point of view is third person limited.

The author is establishing the setting and conflict of the story. Santa Barbara is a country on the brink of civil war, with the Whites and the Reds on opposite sides. The narrator's goal is to introduce the reader to the situation and to establish the main characters and their relationships.
