The Big Six are searching for a lost child, Betsy, in the woods. They are divided into pairs and sent in different directions. Maida and Arthur are one of the pairs. They search thoroughly but do not find her. They return to the Little House and telephone the Big House.

The purpose of this scene is to create suspense and tension as the characters search for a missing child. The author achieves this by having the characters search in different directions, making it clear that the woods are large and Betsy could be anywhere. The characters are also worried that they may not find Betsy before it gets dark.

The style of this scene is tense and suspenseful, with a sense of foreboding as the characters search for the missing child. The point of view is third person limited, focusing on the thoughts and feelings of Maida. The tone is anxious and worried.
