- Honora Cassidy leaves her estate to the man who marries her half-brother's child, Annie Clancy, within 30 days of her death
- If Annie Clancy does not marry within that time frame, the estate goes to the only man who ever proposed to Honora Cassidy (and who she refused)

The purpose of this scene is to reveal the contents of Honora Cassidy's will, which stipulates that her estate will go to the man who marries her half-brother's child, Annie Clancy, within 30 days of her death. This creates a sense of urgency for Larry, who is determined to win Annie's hand. The author also achieves a sense of humor and suspense through the dialogue and characters' reactions.

The literary style of this scene is light and humorous. The tone is playful and the point of view is third person omniscient.
