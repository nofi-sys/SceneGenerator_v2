Rawson, Bassett, and Williams are questioning Anne Tracy about her missing friend, Joe Tracy. Anne insists that Joe is innocent and that he must have left the island for some other reason. Rawson reveals that they have searched the island and cannot find him. Anne becomes agitated and insists that Joe is not a murderer.

The purpose of this scene is to establish conflict and tension between the characters. The author achieves this by having the protagonist, Anne, defend the character of Joe Tracy against the accusations of the other characters.
