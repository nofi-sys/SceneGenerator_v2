In this scene, the protagonist, Manton, is reading a letter from a fan of his work. The letter is full of high praise for Manton's latest poem. Manton is initially pleased by the letter, but then he starts to doubt the sincerity of the praise. He becomes angry and decides to stop reading the fan's letters.

The purpose of this scene is to establish the character of Manton and to provide background information on the poem that he has written. The author achieves these goals by having Manton react to a letter he has received from a fan of his poem.

The literary style of this scene is characterized by its use of harsh, direct language. There is also a sense of urgency and intensity in the way Manton reads the letter and poem.
