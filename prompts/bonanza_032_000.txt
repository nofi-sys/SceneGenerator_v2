-Father Marston and Dutch are walking to Benton's stable
-They are discussing Dutch's reputation
-Suddenly, a man appears and starts shooting at them
-Dutch is mortally wounded
-The man is revealed to be Hugh McClintock
-Dutch dies and Marston comforts him

The style of this scene is tense and suspenseful, with a hint of dark humor. The point of view is third person omniscient.

The purpose of this scene is to show the death of the desperado Sam Dutch. The author achieves this goal by having Dutch die in a gunfight with the singer.
