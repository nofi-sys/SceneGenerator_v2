-Topham: navy officer, en route to Tokio, going via Berlin to see an old friend
-Miss Ferreira: daughter of a German mother and a Brazilian father, returning from a trip to Rio Janiero
-Herrman Ferreira: Miss Ferreira's brother, tall and strong
-Baroness Ostersacken: Miss Ferreira's cousin, plays propriety

The literary style of this scene is formal and polite. The tone is respectful and reserved. The point of view is third person limited.

Topham is a navy officer who has been ordered to deliver a packet to Berlin. He meets Miss Ferreira on the voyage and falls in love with her, but he knows he cannot speak to her while he is bound for Japan. On the last evening of the voyage, he sees her brother coming on board and resolves to part from her without a word of love.
