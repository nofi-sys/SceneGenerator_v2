The purpose of this scene is to establish the character of the artist and to provide background information on the Titanic disaster. The author achieves these goals by having the artist discuss the Titanic with the narrator.

- Artist and protagonist are marine painters who have become friends
- They discuss the Titanic disaster and possible ways to prevent it from happening again
- The protagonist suggests a detachable upper deck, but the artist points out the many flaws in the plan
- They discuss a new invention that uses inaudible sound to detect icebergs, but neither of them are sure it will work

1. The artist - a marine painter, older man, friendly with the narrator
2. Old Bill - feeble-minded man who cleans studios and poses for artists, talks to the narrator in the evenings
3. The narrator - a younger marine painter, friendly with the artist
