Ruth: protagonist, mother, wife of Rowan
Falkner: antagonist, convict, shot Gilroy
Governor McDowell: kind, hospitable, firm
Warden: curt
Rowan (junior): Ruth's son

The purpose of this scene is to show the reader the lengths that Ruth will go to in order to get her husband out of prison. She is willing to sacrifice anything, even her own happiness, in order to achieve her goal. The author also shows the reader the harsh reality of life in prison, and the effect it can have on a person's mind and body.

Ruth visits Falkner in prison in an attempt to get him to help her husband Rowan get out on parole. Falkner is uncooperative and hostile, and tells her that he would rather see her and her husband rot than help them.
