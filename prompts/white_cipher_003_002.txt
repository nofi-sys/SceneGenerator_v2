Fay visits an ex-fence to change some stolen Bank of England notes. The ex-fence, Clanson, is reluctant but takes the money. Fay then asks about a woman named Saidee Isaacs. Clanson says he saw her in a motor car in Cockspur Street and Piccadilly. Fay leaves in a hurry.

-Fay: a criminal from the west of London; ex-fence; has a collection of Japanese and Javanese daggers
-Clanson: an ex-fence; has a strong-box; once said, "If there were no receivers there would be no thieves"
-Sir Richard: gave Fay the ten-pound notes to change
-Edward: one of Fay's many aliases
-Gus: Dutch Gus; a "rat"
-Saidee Isaacs: a lady with a motor car

The literary style of this scene is tense and suspenseful. The point of view is third person limited, and the tone is one of menace and danger.
