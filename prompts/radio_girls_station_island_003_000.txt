Mr. Drew and Mr. Norwood discuss the matter of Hackle Island, which belongs to Henrietta and Bertha. Jessie's father is interested in helping the girls prove their ownership of the island. Mr. Norwood is considering renting a bungalow on the island.

The purpose of this scene is to introduce the conflict over Hackle Island and to establish the characters' positions in the conflict. The author achieves this by having the fathers of Jessie and Amy discuss the matter in front of their daughters, who are interested in the outcome because of their friendship with Henrietta.

The style of this scene is conversational and matter-of-fact. The tone is curious and interested, but not excited. The point of view is third person omniscient.
