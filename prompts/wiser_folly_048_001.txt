Elizabeth: She is the child in false surroundings. She is also the new strength in him, the new decision. There is a curious air of confidence about her, also a certain indefinable joyousness.
David: He is the leader of the expedition. He has taken complete control of it. He is in his natural element. He is also the child in false surroundings.

The purpose of this scene is to establish the setting and to introduce the characters. The author achieves this by describing the surroundings in detail and by showing the characters interacting with each other.

Elizabeth and David come to a stop and look around. They don't see anyone. David goes to the river and looks around. He says that they should go upstream. Elizabeth agrees. They walk upstream and find a piece of paper. They follow the paper and find a tree-trunk. They cross the tree-trunk and find a gate.
