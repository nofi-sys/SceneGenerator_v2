-Dicky: disconsolate figure watching the others swim; poor swimmer
-Arthur: superior size and strength; superior skill; gives Dicky a swimming lesson
-Harold: friend of Arthur's; good swimmer
-Laura: good swimmer; displays water tricks
-Maida: not a fast or strong swimmer, but can swim for a long time; tells a story
-Rosie: naughtiest little girl in the world; good swimmer; has lots of energy

- Dicky is on the pier, while the others swim
- Arthur gives Dicky a swimming lesson
- Laura displays a number of water tricks
- Arthur sets off alone for the middle of the pond

The purpose of this scene is to introduce the reader to the characters and to establish the setting. The author also establishes the conflict in this scene - the children are divided between those who can swim and those who cannot.
