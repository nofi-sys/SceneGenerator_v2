The style of this scene is light and humorous. The point of view is third person omniscient, and the tone is playful.

- John hears a sound and sees a girl coming towards him
- He hides and watches as she goes to the circle of stones and finds the thimble, ribbon, and rosary
- She prays with the rosary and then leaves
- John is surprised to see two knights and a woman behind him

The purpose of this scene is to introduce the character of Molly Biddulph and to establish her as a young girl who is devout in her Catholic faith. The author also uses this scene to establish the setting as being in England.
