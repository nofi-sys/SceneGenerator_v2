The purpose of this scene is to establish the relationship between the two main characters, Alice and Kelsey. Alice is trying to sell herself to Kelsey, and she is hoping that he will be interested in her. The author achieves this goal by having Alice imagine a conversation with Kelsey, in which she tries to find out what he is reading. This shows that she is interested in him and is trying to find a way to connect with him.

- Alice is walking to Kelsey's house, thinking about how to sell herself to him
- She plans to pretend to be looking for someone's address as an excuse to talk to him
- When Kelsey answers the door, Alice is immediately drawn to him
- Kelsey tells her she is five blocks from the address she is looking for, and Alice steps closer to him
- Alice realizes she doesn't need to pretend anymore, and Kelsey realizes who she is

The style of this scene is very formal and stilted. The point of view is third person omniscient, and the tone is one of polite interest.
