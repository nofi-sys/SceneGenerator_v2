- Jessie and her friends Amy and Nell are sitting in Jessie's house in the Roselawn district of New Melford when they hear a commotion outside.
- They go to investigate and find that it is Henrietta Haney, a child from the Dogtown district, who is causing the commotion.
- Henrietta is very excited and tells Jessie that she has something wonderful to tell her.

The purpose of this scene is to introduce the characters of Henrietta and Montmorency and to establish their friendship. The author also establishes the setting of the story and introduces the conflict (the poverty of Dogtown versus the wealth of Roselawn).

The style of this scene is light and humorous. The tone is playful and the point of view is third person limited.
