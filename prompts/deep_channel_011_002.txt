The purpose of this scene is to establish a connection between the two characters and to create a sense of empathy between them. The author achieves this by having the two characters share their fears and doubts with each other.

A man tells a woman that he was thinking about the boy who was sent back from camp, and how he might be laughed at. The woman says she would never laugh at him. The man then confesses that he was thinking about how he might be laughed at next week. The woman reassures him that she understands.

The literary style of this scene is characterized by its intense emotional honesty. The protagonist is in a state of extreme vulnerability, and he bares his soul to the reader without any pretense or artifice. This rawness gives the scene an almost painful intimacy, as we feel privy to his every thought and feeling.
