The purpose of this scene is to reveal the character of Annie and to establish the conflict in the story. Annie is a young woman who is married to a much younger man. She is shy and reserved, but also has a strong resolve. The conflict in the story is between Annie and her husband, Harry.

- Harry is leaving for a fortnight and writes Annie a strange letter
- Annie is still weak from illness, but is amused and touched by the letter
- George is kind to her and William dances around her in joy
- Lilian is getting married and George offers to take Annie and his mother to Southport after the wedding
- On the day before Harry is due back, Annie announces she is going to her aunts' house

The style of this scene is formal and somewhat stilted. The point of view is third person omniscient.
