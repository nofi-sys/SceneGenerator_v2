The purpose of this scene is to establish the rivalry between the two candidates for secretary of state, Scot McClintock and Ralph Dodson. The author also establishes the character of each man and the reasons why each is supported.

The literary style of this scene is characterized by its use of third person point of view, its formal tone, and its focus on the political campaign.

- The scene is set during a political campaign in which Scot McClintock and Ralph Dodson are running against each other for the position of secretary of state.
- Scot is a popular figure with a lot of individual support, while Dodson is backed by the big mining interests.
- Dodson starts to spread rumors about Scot in an attempt to discredit him.
- Vicky meets Dodson and tells him off for his lies.
- Dodson agrees to deny the rumors in the paper.
