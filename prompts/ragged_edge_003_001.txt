- A man is dying, and his grandson has come to see him
- The man is delirious and asks for his daughter, Mary
- The attorney is there to witness his will
- The man leaves his money to Mary

The purpose of this scene is to establish the setting and to introduce the characters. The author achieves this by describing the house and the people in it.

- Larry: young, gruff, angry
- Kelly: stout, heavy-browed, sneering
- the girl: tall, slim, coughing, with bright spots on her cheeks
- Father Dawson: priestly-looking young man
- the sick man: parchment-faced, wasted by disease, with gaunt hands
- Rosie OHara: kneeling, head bent, eyes fixed on the floor
- Young Kerrigan: sitting, reading a newly written paper
