The purpose of this scene is to establish the characters of William and Annie. William is a high-spirited boy who is careless and doesn't always think things through. Annie is a more level-headed girl who is trying to make the best of her situation. The author also establishes the setting of the story and the conflict between the two characters.

-Annie: protagonist, young woman who has left her husband
-William: boy from the Grange, friends with Annie
-Harry: Annie's husband, thought to be in Leicestershire
-The showily-dressed woman: unknown, caught Harry's eye

A woman named Annie sees a man who looks like her husband, Harry, and follows him into the station. It turns out to be Harry, and he is staring at a showily-dressed woman. Annie is proud of him and feels a pang of regret that she is leaving him forever.
