Muckle John escapes capture by the English, leaving behind a boy, Rob Fraser, who is caught and taken to Fort Augustus.

The style of this scene is tense and suspenseful, with a hint of humor. The point of view is third person omniscient.

The purpose of this scene is to introduce the character of Muckle John and to establish his reputation as a dangerous rebel. The author also establishes the character of Rob Fraser as a boy who is in over his head.
