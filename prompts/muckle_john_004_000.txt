- Rob is in the Highlands of Scotland, staying with the old and sick Lord Lovat. 
- One morning, Lovat tasks Rob with going into the hills to see where the English soldiers are and whether a French frigate is offshore. 
- Rob sets out and, as evening falls, comes upon a beach where there is a frigate anchored offshore. 
- Rob hides and watches as people on shore load cargo from the frigate onto the shore. 
- Suddenly, the frigate takes off and an English man-of-war is firing at it.

The purpose of this scene is to establish the setting and to introduce the conflict. The author achieves this by describing the landscape and the characters in the scene.

The tone of this scene is one of suspense and excitement, as the reader is left wondering what will happen next. The point of view is third person limited, as we only know what Rob sees and hears. The style is straightforward and simple, with short sentences and clear descriptions.
