The literary style of this scene is light-hearted and humorous. The tone is playful and the point of view is third person omniscient.

The purpose of this scene is to introduce the conflict and to establish the setting. The author achieves this by introducing the characters and the situation.

- Ethel and Teddy are talking about the man who threw a cigarette at Teddy
- They are waiting for a car to come pick them up
- A car pulls up and it is full of dust
- A man gets out of the car and starts talking to the group
- The man is late because he had a puncture and he is driving a "hopper-necked, sawed off, lead mule"
- Nell and Ethel are trying not to laugh
- Mr. Manley is going to ride back with them
- Mr. Manley walks down the street
- The two boys are waiting for their father
- Mr. Manley comes back and tells them that he found out that their horses were stolen
