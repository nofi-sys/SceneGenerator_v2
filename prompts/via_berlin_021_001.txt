The style of this scene is tense and suspenseful. The point of view is third person limited, and the tone is one of foreboding.

The purpose of this scene is to establish the setting and to introduce the conflict. The author achieves this by describing the city and the crowd, and by introducing the character of Topham.

-Topham is looking for a restaurant on Market Street in San Francisco when he is stopped by a man telling him there is a strike and he shouldn't go into the Japanese restaurant he is looking for.
-Topham pushes past the man and goes into the restaurant anyway, where he recognizes the owner as a Colonel in the Japanese army.
-Suddenly, a crowd of men rush into the restaurant, forcing the two policemen at the door back with their clubs.
