The purpose of this scene is to introduce the character of Lissa and to establish her as an expert horsewoman. The author also establishes the setting as a prairie with prairie dogs.

- Lissa wants to ride a horse instead of sitting in the wagon
- Nathan and Mark are hesitant because the horse is wild, but Lissa is an expert horsewoman
- They horse, named Whirlwind, takes off at a fast pace and Lissa can't slow him down
- They pass a prairie-dog town and the horse slows down
- They reach their destination and Lissa is reunited with her sister

The style of this scene is conversational and light-hearted. The tone is cheerful and the point of view is third person limited.
