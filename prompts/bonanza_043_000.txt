Years later, he is recognized as a drunken wreck in a San Francisco dive, and rumour has it that he died soon after in Mexico. The Dodson fortune collapses with the death of Ralph. Hugh McClintock, meanwhile, has been summoned out of town on business and returns to find Vicky. They walk together and Hugh confesses his love for her.

The purpose of this scene is to establish the relationship between Hugh and Vicky. The author achieves this by having them reminisce about their shared history and by showing their deep connection to each other.

The style of this scene is romantic and nostalgic. The tone is wistful and longing, and the point of view is from the perspective of the man looking back on his life.
