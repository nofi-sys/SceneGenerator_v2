The purpose of this scene is to establish the setting and to introduce the characters. The author achieves this by describing the canal and the ship, and by introducing the two main characters, Fay and Saidee.

The literary style of this scene is tense and suspenseful. The point of view is third person limited, and the tone is one of anxiety and fear.

- Scene is set in England, near a canal
- Two people, Chester and Saidee, are talking in a summer house
- They discuss a cipher that Chester has decoded, and plan to deliver it to Sir Richard
- They hear a noise outside and see a shadow moving in the canal
- Chester hides the cipher in the summer house and they go to investigate
- They see a ship and Fay tells Saidee to go aboard while he gets the bags
- Fay returns to the summer house and is met by two men
- The men take Fay away and the scene ends
