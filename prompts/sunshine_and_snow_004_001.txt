The purpose of this scene is to create suspense and excitement as the characters race against time to beat an avalanche. The author achieves this by describing the dangerous conditions of the race and the stakes if they fail.

The literary style of this scene is characterized by its tense, suspenseful tone. The point of view is from the perspective of the characters in the scene, and the action is described in detail, making it feel as though the reader is right there with them.

- Arthur and Charley Gordon are told by a man that a big snow-slide is coming which will destroy the railway they are building.
- They run to the construction camp to try and stop it and find some men to help them.
- They succeed in finishing the snow shed just in time and the avalanche is stopped.
