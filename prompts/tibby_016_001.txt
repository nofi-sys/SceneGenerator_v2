- Donald is at a séance with his sister-in-law, Mrs. McCleary, and her friends. 
- Mrs. McCleary is talking to the group about her son, George, and how he was cured of a fever by taking pills that appeared to him in a vision. 
- Auntie Dearborn arrives and says that she has been inspired by the spirit of Lord Byron to write poetry. 
- The group forms a circle and the séance begins.

The tone of this scene is light and humorous. The point of view is third person omniscient.

The purpose of this scene is to introduce the character of Auntie Dearborn and to establish her as a eccentric old lady who believes that the spirits of dead poets use her as a medium to present their work to the public.
