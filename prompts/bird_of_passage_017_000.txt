-Helen Denis: main character, shy, artist
-Mrs. Creery: old, wealthy woman, rude
-Mr. Lisle: wealthy man, skilled artist

- Helen is trying to sketch a landscape but is being constantly interrupted by her mother, who is critiquing her work.
- Mrs. Creery sees a boat coming and assumes it is Mr. Manners, but is disappointed to see that it is Mr. Lisle.
- Mr. Lisle is about to leave but decides to stay when he hears that Helen is also on the boat.
- He looks at her sketch and makes a few quick changes that greatly improve it.

The purpose of this scene is to establish the characters of Mr. Lisle and Mrs. Creery, and to set up the conflict between them. The author also uses the scene to establish the setting and to introduce the plot.
