The style of this scene is formal and somewhat stilted. The point of view is third person limited, and the tone is one of polite conversation.

- Stites, secretary of legation in Tokio, and Topham, a new arrival to the country, sit at a table in the English Hotel and drink wine. 
- Stites tells Topham that he is glad to have him, as they need skilled men for the embassy, and Topham is a licensed navy man. 
- Topham says he could have come quicker, but he was needed as watch officer on the Nevada. 
- Stites tells him that the need for him has been increasing, as they mean to fight the Japanese, and they have been grossly negligent in preparing.

The purpose of this scene is to introduce the reader to the setting and to the two main characters. The author also establishes the conflict in this scene, which is that the two men are discussing the preparations for war with Japan.
