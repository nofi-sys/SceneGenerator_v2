The style of this scene is tense and suspenseful. The point of view is third person limited, and the tone is one of urgency.

-Fay: the protagonist; a skilled robber and escape artist
-The German deserter: a drunken man who is bribed by Fay to help him get a car
-The Holland maid: a girl who works at the inn and serves Fay beer
-The Dutch boy: a boy who drives the flivver (a small, old car) that Fay escapes in

Fay, a robber, escapes on a ship to Holland. He finds a bar and asks the German deserter if he knows of any cars. The German leaves and comes back with a Dutch boy in a car. Fay tells the boy to drive him to the other side of the country.
